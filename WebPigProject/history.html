History of Pigs - the Dice game



First written by John Scarne in 1945 Pigs is a simple folk dice game which has had many different renditions and slight rule changes. The basic core of the game is featured throughout many different renditions and names. The goal of the game is to roll a score of 100. The most common variant (the one played here) is accomplished by rolling a six sided die. After each roll a player adds the total to their temporary points. Then they can decided to roll again and attempt to gain more points or pass their turn and convert all the current turns temporary points to permanent points.
The catch is if at any point the player rolls a one then they forfeit all their temporary points and pass there turn to the other player.

The games simplicity combined with a great deal of luck and strategy allowed remain popular especially in rural areas for quite some time. Other variants of the game rolling two dice, with different outcomes for doubles and other variances also became popular.

The inherent math probability concepts lead it to be a common tool for math teachers, elementary students could play it to practice addition. Later it became a strong tool in the use of teaching artificial intelligence. This causes the computer to balances gaining optional scoring with their opponents progress in the game.

Weather it a game for learning or just for fun the simplicity of pigs is fun for everyone
