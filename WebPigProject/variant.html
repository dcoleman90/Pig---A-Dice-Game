The many different versions of Pig



Due to a number of factors, most centering upon the simplicity and rural nature of the game Pigs, many different spin-off versions of the game have been created.

Most of these versions involve adding a second dice and differing the rules based upon the second dice outcome. In the Two-Dice variant, players add both dice sums to their total. If either dice results in a one their turn is forfeit, if both dice are ones then they lose all their points (even the stored ones) and have to end their turn at a value of 0. Some versions compel players who roll doubles to roll again, rather than bank their points. One popular version known as Big Pig is more forgiving and on upon rolling doubles gives the player twice the value rolled, and on double ones grants them a score of twenty-five

Skunk is a group version of two dice pig where players play a predetermined five rounds. All player bet on one set of dice and may choose to hold at any time. The pair of dice are continuously rolled until a one appears, or everyone holds. At this point everyone who has not held forfeits all points from the round. The winner is the player with the highest number of points at the end of five rounds

Other versions involve reactions between certain predetermined outcomes. Most of these are house rules and take effect when certain unlikely outcomes arise, such as rolling doubles multiple times in a row.
